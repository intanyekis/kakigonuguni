    Biography

    Ali Bongo Ondimba was born in Brazzaville, Congo Republic on February 9, 1959. Congo-born Gabonese politician known for being the president of Gabon, a position he was elected to in 2009 after his father, Omar Bongo, died in office. He’d previously served as Minister of Defense. He met Barack Obama at the White House in 2011. He attended Pantheon-Sorbonne University in France before entering politics. He joined the Gabonese Democratic Party in 1981 and would eventually become president of the party.

    Career

    He is a Gabonese politician who has been Gabon’s third president since October 2009.

    Ali Bongo is the son of Omar Bongo, who was President of Gabon from 1967 to his death in 2009. During his father’s presidency, he was Minister of Foreign Affairs from 1989 to 1991, represented Bongoville as Deputy of the National Assembly from 1991 to 1999, and served as Minister of Defense from 1999 to 2009. After his father died, he won the Gabonese presidential election in 2009.  In 2016, he was re-elected in elections marred by numerous irregularities, arrests, human rights violations and post-election protests, and violence. Bongo is also president of the Democratic Party of Gabon.
